About
     

        I've been in the DIY scene long enough to have opinions about it. I do lights — mostly because I love it and partly because someone has to. I've run rigs for punk shows, queer dance nights, experimental electronic sets, and everything in between, across spaces that were not designed for any of this.      

     

        I'm also a veteran, a psychology grad, and someone who has spent a lot of time thinking about community resilience, emergency communication, and what it actually means to prepare — not in a prepper way, in a "my friends need real information" way. That's where Grid Down, Still Here comes from.      

     

        I work with communities that are often targeted, often broke, and often overlooked by the systems that claim to help. I'm not going to pretend AI is magic or that technology solves political problems. But I do think access to good information and good tools — used by people who trust each other — is part of what keeps us okay. REAL ONE

Common questions
     
       
What do you charge?
       
$150–$400 per show depending on load-in complexity, duration, and how far I'm traveling. I'm not trying to be your most expensive line item.
     
     
       
Do you bring your own rig?
       
I work with what your venue has. If you have nothing, we can talk about what's realistic. I don't have a truck full of fixtures — yet.
     
     
       
What genres do you work with?
       
Punk, metal, electronic, noise, experimental, hip-hop, queer club nights. I'm comfortable in weird spaces. Trad country wedding: probably not your person.
     
   
   
     
More questions
     
       
About Grid Down, Still Here?
       
It's a free resource. Pay what you can via Ko-fi. The information is for everyone — no gatekeeping. Updates ongoing.
     
     
       
Are you suspicious of AI?
       
I understand if you are. I use it as a tool, same as I'd use anything else. Nothing on this page was generated without my voice and my choices in it.
     
     
       
What if my budget is very small?
       
Talk to me anyway. I'm not going to price out the scene I actually care about. We'll figure something out or I'll be honest with you.
